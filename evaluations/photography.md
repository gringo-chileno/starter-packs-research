# Photography & Visual Arts Starter Pack Evaluation

Evaluated: April 13, 2026

## Scoring Rubric

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 20-25 | Strong pick, prioritize for outreach |
| 15-19 | Acceptable, keep unless a better option exists |
| 10-14 | Weak, look for replacement |
| Below 10 | Replace immediately |

---

## 1. Streets of Nuremberg (streetsofnuremberg.com) -- 24/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 5 | Excellent original street photography with sophisticated technique. Thoughtful, well-written accompaniment about philosophy and context. Posts like "Moves" (chess players) and "Faces of the Street" (Genoa portraits) show technical skill and narrative depth. |
| Reader Appeal | 4 | Compelling for anyone interested in photography or visual storytelling. Consistent monochrome aesthetic creates a distinctive feed identity. ~3 posts/month. |
| Brand Safety | 5 | Clean. Street photography, travel, contemplative observations about urban life. |
| Fediverse Fit | 5 | Strong personal voice, genuine community interaction (responds meaningfully to all comments), shares technical knowledge freely. "Learning Center" for street photography tips shows community-building instincts. |
| Visual Polish | 5 | Clean, minimalist dark theme that perfectly serves the monochrome photography. Professional layout with generous image spacing. |

**Verdict: Keep. Best blog in the pack.**

---

## 2. PostSecret (postsecret.com) -- 21/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 5 | Entirely original, user-generated art. Culturally significant project (20+ years). Each postcard is a unique mixed-media piece. Featured on CBS Sunday Morning. |
| Reader Appeal | 5 | Deeply compelling, emotionally resonant. Weekly "Sunday Secrets" format creates anticipation. One of the most famous blog projects in existence. |
| Brand Safety | 2 | **Concern.** The "Dark Matter" longform piece discusses suicide, self-harm, abuse, and explicit content. Regular postcards touch on mental health crises, infidelity, substance abuse. Content is inherently unpredictable since it's user-submitted anonymous confessions. Handled with care, but Automattic cannot control what themes appear week to week. |
| Fediverse Fit | 4 | The project is fundamentally about human connection and sharing. Frank Warren maintains a distinctive curatorial presence. Low posting frequency (6 posts in 2026). |
| Visual Polish | 5 | Clean, ad-free, minimalist dark design that showcases the postcard art beautifully. |

**Verdict: DECISION NEEDED.** Extraordinary content, but the brand safety score is a genuine concern. PostSecret regularly features secrets about suicide, abuse, addiction, and explicit themes. The team should decide whether they're comfortable recommending a feed with unpredictable heavy content.

## 3. This, That & the Other Thing (sustainabilitea.wordpress.com) -- 19/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 3 | Original photography with personal reflections. Pleasant but not exceptional. Many posts are short (single photo with a few words for challenge participation). |
| Reader Appeal | 3 | Gentle, pleasant content. High posting frequency (47 posts in ~3.5 months) but much of it is lightweight. |
| Brand Safety | 5 | Clean. Nature photography, travel, language musings. |
| Fediverse Fit | 5 | Perfect fediverse blog. Personal voice, community participation (weekly challenges), genuine engagement with other bloggers, conversational tone. Exactly what "blogging as social activity" looks like. |
| Visual Polish | 3 | Standard WordPress.com template. The photography carries the visual weight. |

**Verdict: Keep.** Solid representative of the personal photography blogging community. Deep community engagement makes it an excellent fediverse fit.

## 4. Kevin Standage Photography (kevinstandagephotography.wordpress.com) -- 19/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 5 | Exceptional depth and originality. The Belur Temple post documents multiple structures with architectural measurements, historical context, and comprehensive photography. Delhi alone has 100+ specific monument entries. |
| Reader Appeal | 3 | Niche appeal (Indian heritage, architecture, travel photography). Academic/reference style limits broad appeal. Low posting frequency (6 posts in 2026). |
| Brand Safety | 5 | Clean. Respectful cultural and architectural documentation. |
| Fediverse Fit | 2 | Functions more as a reference archive than a personal blog. Scholarly, documentary style lacks conversational voice. |
| Visual Polish | 4 | Clean WordPress.com theme with well-organized hierarchical navigation. Heavy on image galleries. |

**Verdict: Keep.** Impressive body of work. The least "social" blog in the pack, but content quality and diversity of perspective justify inclusion.

---

## 5. The Frugal Crafter (thefrugalcrafter.wordpress.com) -- 17/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 3 | Mixed. Some genuine tutorial elements, but many posts are sales pitches for paid courses. The "Getting Started with Gouache" post was ~85% promotional. Actual instruction lives in embedded YouTube videos rather than blog posts. |
| Reader Appeal | 3 | Appealing to crafters who follow Lindsay. But heavy promotional content (course sales, affiliate links, discount codes) reduces value for casual followers. |
| Brand Safety | 5 | Clean. Family-friendly art and craft content. |
| Fediverse Fit | 3 | Lindsay has a friendly voice ("Happy crafting!"). "Sat Chat" format is community-oriented. But the heavy commercial angle conflicts with fediverse's community-first ethos. |
| Visual Polish | 3 | Functional WordPress.com blog with standard template. Heavy with embedded videos and sidebar promotions. |

**Verdict: Keep (lowest in pack).** Genuine WordPress.com creator with a real audience, which is a plus. Main weakness is heavy promotional content.
