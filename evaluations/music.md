# Music Starter Pack Evaluation

Evaluated: April 13, 2026

## Scoring Rubric

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 20-25 | Strong pick, prioritize for outreach |
| 15-19 | Acceptable, keep unless a better option exists |
| 10-14 | Weak, look for replacement |
| Below 10 | Replace immediately |

---

## 1. Song of the Day for Today (songoftheday.ca) -- 24/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 5 | Exceptional original writing. Posts run 1,500-3,000 words with deep biographical research, lyrical analysis, and personal storytelling. The Jeff Buckley post was a masterclass in music blogging. |
| Reader Appeal | 5 | Daily cadence is perfect for a feed. Eclectic genre coverage (classical, rock, pop, folk). Guest contributors add variety. The "Under the Covers" series shows creative editorial thinking. |
| Brand Safety | 5 | Clean. Wholesome personal blog. The author writes about his wife, cycling, and Winnipeg sunrises. |
| Fediverse Fit | 5 | Strong personal voice ("Steve" is a real character you get to know). Conversational, community-oriented with guest posts and a loyal commenting audience. |
| Visual Polish | 4 | Clean, readable layout with white background and good typography. Embedded YouTube videos enhance posts. |

**Verdict: Keep. Crown jewel of the pack.**

---

## 2. mvm / Man v Music (manvmusic.com) -- 20/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 4 | Solid original music criticism. Distinctive descriptive style. Albums of the Month roundups are well-curated. Covers interesting ground (Enter Shikari, RAYE, French-Canadian math-rock). |
| Reader Appeal | 4 | Good mix of album reviews, artist features, and roundups. Posts every few days. |
| Brand Safety | 4 | Generally clean. One mild concern: a Nigel Farage metaphor in a review is slightly political but not partisan. |
| Fediverse Fit | 4 | Clear editorial voice and personality. Feels like one person's genuine opinions. Good fediverse energy: opinionated and conversational. |
| Visual Polish | 4 | Clean, modern design with "Overused Grotesk" typeface, cream background, grid layouts. Professional and readable. |

**Verdict: Keep.** Solid indie music publication. Alt/indie/post-punk focus complements Song of the Day's broader coverage.

## 3. Gimmie Gimmie Gimmie Zine (gimmiezine.com) -- 20/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 4 | Original, in-depth Q&A interviews with musicians from Melbourne punk bands to Guitar Wolf's Seiji. Interviews run 400-4,500 words with thoughtful questions. Professional photography. |
| Reader Appeal | 3 | Engaging but niche (deep Australian underground scene). Only 9 posts in 2026. Inconsistent cadence outside of the JERKFEST burst. |
| Brand Safety | 5 | Clean. Independent music zine celebrating underground artists. Slightly edgy content (punk bands) but nothing concerning. |
| Fediverse Fit | 4 | Strong indie/DIY ethos aligns with fediverse values. Original content, community focus, supporting independent artists. |
| Visual Polish | 4 | Clean, minimalist design with professional photography. Oswald font headings. Well-executed. |

**Verdict: Keep.** Good original journalism. Low post count (9 in 2026) is a concern to monitor. Interview format offers something different from the review-focused blogs.

## 4. ~~Kenta's...Nothing But Pop!~~ (kenta45rpm.com) -- 19/25 REPLACED

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 4 | Thoughtful, knowledgeable music reviews with personal context. Author weaves in details about his life (book promotion, travel). |
| Reader Appeal | 3 | **Critical issue: The blog is entirely in Japanese.** Post titles include English artist names, but all commentary is Japanese. Purchase links point to Japanese retailers (Tower Records, Rakuten). |
| Brand Safety | 5 | Clean. Personal music criticism. |
| Fediverse Fit | 4 | Excellent personal voice, conversational, authentic. But the language barrier is a fundamental problem. |
| Visual Polish | 3 | Functional traditional blog layout. Dated design. |

**Verdict: Replaced.** Strong metrics (587 subs, 33K views) and genuine quality writing, but the blog is entirely in Japanese. For an English-language starter pack, recommending a blog most followers cannot read is a poor user experience. Would be an excellent inclusion in a future Japanese-language pack.

### Replacement: Brutally Honest Rock Album Reviews (brutallyhonestrockalbumreviews.wordpress.com)

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 4 | Opinionated, knowledgeable rock album reviews. Tagline: "WHERE THE TRUTH IS UNVARNISHED, NO ONE GETS AN UNEARNED GOOD REVIEW." 80+ artists reviewed. Covers classic and modern rock (Jimmy Page & Robert Plant, Van Halen, Paul McCartney & Wings). |
| Reader Appeal | 4 | Fun contrarian angle. Provocative titles ("Yeah, I'm afraid Animals just isn't a great album..."). Wide catalog of reviews. |
| Brand Safety | 4 | Opinions are directed at albums, not people. Contrarian but not mean-spirited. |
| Fediverse Fit | 4 | Strong personal voice. Irreverent tone is perfect for fediverse engagement and debate. WordPress.com hosted. |
| Visual Polish | 4 | Clean WordPress site with good typography. Serif headings, professional layout. Comprehensive archive table. |
| **Total** | **20** | |

Stats: 20,261 30-day views, 218 subscribers, 11 posts in 2026, last post Apr 9. WordPress.com hosted.

## 5. HighClouds (highclouds.org) -- 18/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 3 | Posts are short (200-400 words) and lean toward press-release-adjacent announcements rather than deep analysis. "Hype Machine-listed" is a credential, but writing feels formulaic. |
| Reader Appeal | 3 | Good for indie music discovery. Multiple posts per week covering emerging artists. But brief announcements don't give readers reason to engage deeply. Better as a link aggregator than a must-read. |
| Brand Safety | 5 | Clean. Explicitly supports LGBTQ+ and feminist artists ("Homocore" section). Brussels-based. |
| Fediverse Fit | 2 | Reads like a media publication, not a personal blog. "A team of music lovers" runs it, but individual voices are absent. Posts feel like curated PR. |
| Visual Polish | 5 | Excellent design with pastel pink/purple palette, generous whitespace, modern typography. Best-looking site in the pack. |

**Verdict: Keep (weakest in pack).** The 4-subscriber count likely reflects Jetpack (real audience invisible to WP.com API). Competent but formulaic. If a stronger candidate surfaces, replace this first.
