# Notable Authors Starter Pack Evaluation

Evaluated: April 13, 2026

## Scoring Rubric

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 20-25 | Strong pick, prioritize for outreach |
| 15-19 | Acceptable, keep unless a better option exists |
| 10-14 | Weak, look for replacement |
| Below 10 | Replace immediately |

---

## 1. Seth's Blog (seths.blog) -- 24/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 5 | Daily short-form posts distilling marketing, leadership, and creative thinking into sharp insights. Seth Godin is one of the most influential marketing thinkers alive. 20+ books including *Purple Cow* and *The Dip*. |
| Reader Appeal | 5 | Perfect feed content: short, daily, consistently thought-provoking. 110 posts in 2026. 276,820 30-day views and 13,887 subscribers confirm massive engaged audience. |
| Brand Safety | 5 | Clean. Positive, constructive tone. No political content. |
| Fediverse Fit | 5 | Minimalist personal voice. Each post reads like a thought you'd share with a friend. Ideal fediverse content: short, opinionated, original. |
| Visual Polish | 4 | Clean, minimal design. Yellow accent color is iconic branding. Functional rather than flashy. |

**Verdict: Keep. Strongest blog in the pack.**

---

## 2. Whatever (whatever.scalzi.com) -- 22/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 5 | John Scalzi is a Hugo Award-winning sci-fi author (*Old Man's War*, *The Kaiju Preservation Society*). Blog mixes personal life (cats, food, sunsets), publishing industry insights, cultural commentary, and politics. One of the longest-running personal blogs on the internet (since 1998). |
| Reader Appeal | 5 | 110 posts in 2026. Wide range of topics keeps the feed interesting. 32,505 subscribers and 97,536 30-day views. The "Big Idea" series (guest authors pitching their new books) is a draw for readers. |
| Brand Safety | 4 | Scalzi is politically outspoken (liberal). Opinions are clearly his own and expressed with humor rather than anger. Occasional strong language. |
| Fediverse Fit | 4 | Strong personal voice, conversational, frequently engages with commenters. Longtime blogger who genuinely enjoys the medium. |
| Visual Polish | 4 | Clean self-hosted WordPress. Professional layout with good typography. |

**Verdict: Keep. Quintessential personal blog from a major author.**

---

## 3. The Bloggess (thebloggess.com) -- 21/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 4 | Jenny Lawson is a NYT bestselling humor/memoir author (*Let's Pretend This Never Happened*, *Furiously Happy*). Blog covers taxidermy, mental health advocacy, humor, and personal stories. Book #4 hit #4 on the NYT bestseller list in April 2026. |
| Reader Appeal | 4 | 14 posts in 2026 (lower frequency). 16,404 subscribers and 91,355 30-day views. Devoted community. Posts are long-form and personal. |
| Brand Safety | 4 | Frank about mental health struggles (depression, anxiety). This is a strength for representation but worth noting. Humor can be dark/absurdist. Nothing offensive. |
| Fediverse Fit | 5 | One of the most community-oriented blogs on this list. Comments section is active and supportive. Lawson's voice is unmistakable: vulnerable, funny, deeply personal. |
| Visual Polish | 4 | Clean WordPress design. Good use of images and personal photography. |

**Verdict: Keep. Strong community voice and mental health advocacy.**

---

## 4. Pluralistic (pluralistic.net) -- 21/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 5 | Cory Doctorow is a sci-fi author (*Little Brother*, *Walkaway*), journalist, and digital rights activist. Coined "enshittification" (2023 Word of the Year, American Dialect Society). Blog covers tech policy, monopoly power, surveillance, and digital rights with deep research and sourcing. |
| Reader Appeal | 4 | 68 posts in 2026. Long, heavily linked posts reward engaged readers. Not casual reading. Already has 74,000+ Mastodon followers, proving fediverse demand. |
| Brand Safety | 4 | Politically engaged (anti-monopoly, pro-privacy). Opinions are well-argued and evidence-based. Strong language directed at corporations and policies, not individuals. |
| Fediverse Fit | 4 | Doctorow is already deeply embedded in the fediverse (active Mastodon presence). The blog cross-posts to multiple platforms. He explicitly advocates for open, federated internet. |
| Visual Polish | 4 | Self-hosted WordPress with distinctive collage-style header images for each post. Clean, readable layout. |

**Verdict: Keep. Already the most fediverse-native author on this list.**

Stats: Subscriber and view counts unavailable via WP.com API (Jetpack site, minimal tracking). Real audience is substantially larger based on 74K+ Mastodon followers.

## 5. Hugh Howey (hughhowey.com) -- 19/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 4 | Author of the *Wool/Silo* series, adapted into the Apple TV+ show *Silo*. Blog posts are philosophical essays on writing, creativity, sailing, and life. Thoughtful and well-written. |
| Reader Appeal | 3 | Only 5 posts in 2026. Low frequency limits feed value. 2,597 subscribers and 14,170 30-day views. When he posts, the writing is strong, but long gaps between posts. |
| Brand Safety | 5 | Clean. Positive, reflective tone. No political content. |
| Fediverse Fit | 4 | Personal, introspective voice. Posts feel like letters from a friend. Good fediverse energy when active. |
| Visual Polish | 3 | Functional WordPress theme. Clean but not distinctive. |

**Verdict: Keep (weakest in pack). Low posting frequency is the main concern. The Silo TV show gives him cultural relevance.**

## Dropped Candidate

### Tim Ferriss (tim.blog) -- 16/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 3 | Blog has shifted almost entirely to podcast episode transcripts and show notes. Original blog writing that made him famous (*4-Hour Workweek* era) is largely absent. |
| Reader Appeal | 3 | Podcast transcripts are long and not optimized for reading. Better consumed as audio. |
| Brand Safety | 5 | Clean. Mainstream self-improvement and business content. |
| Fediverse Fit | 2 | Feels like a media property, not a personal blog. No community interaction. Posts are promotional for the podcast. |
| Visual Polish | 3 | Functional design. Heavy on calls-to-action and newsletter signups. |

**Verdict: Dropped.** The blog no longer functions as a blog. It is a podcast companion site.

## Moonshot: George R.R. Martin (georgerrmartin.com/notablog/)

Not scored because the site is self-hosted WordPress without Jetpack. Cannot enable ActivityPub without plugin installation. Would require direct outreach.

No evidence of follow-through. Team 51 outreach form is available at to51outreach.wordpress.com for a new request.
