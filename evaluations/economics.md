# Economics & Ideas Starter Pack Evaluation

Evaluated: April 13, 2026

## Scoring Rubric

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 20-25 | Strong pick, prioritize for outreach |
| 15-19 | Acceptable, keep unless a better option exists |
| 10-14 | Weak, look for replacement |
| Below 10 | Replace immediately |

---

## 1. Conversable Economist (conversableeconomist.com) -- 24/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 5 | Consistently excellent. Timothy Taylor, managing editor of the Journal of Economic Perspectives (American Economic Association), textbook author. Every post is well-researched and clearly sourced. "North Korea's Economy: How to Study a Data Black Hole" covers satellite imagery and mirror trade statistics as forensic economics tools. |
| Reader Appeal | 5 | Economics you'd actually want in your feed. Topics range from globalization to AI to India's service sector. 49 posts in 2026. Mission: "finding topics of conversation fit for the entertainment of rational creatures" (Hume-inspired). |
| Brand Safety | 5 | Impeccably balanced. Even the tariff analysis ("Predictably Disappointing") presents data and lets readers draw conclusions. No inflammatory language, no partisan attacks. |
| Fediverse Fit | 4 | Strong personal voice. You feel the author's curiosity and intellectual delight. More essayist than conversational. |
| Visual Polish | 5 | Clean, modern self-hosted WordPress. Teal and cream color scheme, Merriweather serif typography, well-integrated charts. Most polished site in the pack. |

**Verdict: Keep. Best blog in the pack by a significant margin.**

---

## 2. The Renaissance Mathematicus (thonyc.wordpress.com) -- 21/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 5 | Exceptional. Deep primary-source scholarship in blog format. The myth-busting niche (correcting popular misconceptions about science history) is genuinely unique. Multi-part series show sustained intellectual commitment. |
| Reader Appeal | 4 | Fascinating for curious readers. The "actually, that's wrong" angle hooks people in. 16 posts in 2026, each substantial. |
| Brand Safety | 4 | Almost no political content. Occasionally harsh in reviews (calling other authors' work "arrant bullshit"), which is blunt but directed at published books, not people. Minor concern at most. |
| Fediverse Fit | 4 | Very strong personal voice. Cranky, passionate, deeply knowledgeable. Personal posts (father's caricature) add warmth. |
| Visual Polish | 4 | Good use of historical portraits and images. Clean WordPress layout with well-organized topic navigation. |

**Verdict: Keep.** Standout blog. Not strictly economics, but "Economics & Ideas" is broad enough to include history of science. Unique voice.

## 3. Michael Roberts Blog (thenextrecession.wordpress.com) -- 18/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 4 | Rigorous data-driven analysis from a clear Marxist framework. Original research (world profit rate calculations), cites IMF, OECD, WTO, Goldman Sachs. 40+ years as a City of London economist, published author. |
| Reader Appeal | 4 | Substantive long-form economics with real-world policy relevance. 27 posts in 2026. Appeals to anyone interested in political economy. |
| Brand Safety | 4 | Explicitly Marxist but academically serious. Criticism of governments is evidence-based, not inflammatory. Even-handed enough to critique both left and right politicians. |
| Fediverse Fit | 3 | Strong personal voice as a single author with decades of expertise. More analytical than conversational. |
| Visual Polish | 3 | Standard WordPress.com theme. Clean and functional but not distinctive. |

**Verdict: Keep.** Serious heterodox economics from a credentialed professional. The Marxist lens is transparent and intellectual, not agitprop.

## 4. ~~Real-World Economics Review~~ (rwer.wordpress.com) -- 13/25 REPLACED

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 3 | Uneven. Some posts (Dean Baker's drug policy analysis) are decent economics. Others are political opinion with thin evidence. Multi-author format means no consistent quality bar. |
| Reader Appeal | 3 | Mix of policy analysis and polemics. Unpredictable quality. |
| Brand Safety | 2 | **Significant concern.** "America's darkest hour" post uses "criminal terrorist nation" and "war crimes trials" language. Multi-author format means weak editorial control. |
| Fediverse Fit | 2 | Institutional journal blog, not a personal voice. Reads more like a content aggregator than a fediverse-native presence. |
| Visual Polish | 3 | Standard blue WordPress theme. Dense text with minimal visual elements. |

**Verdict: Replaced.** Multi-author format is a poor fediverse fit. Brand safety issues with inflammatory political reposts. Useful economics content can be found in the individual authors' own blogs.

### Replacement: Surplus Energy Economics (surplusenergyeconomics.wordpress.com)

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 4 | Tim Morgan's original SEEDS economic model analyzing global economics through an energy lens. "Energy Costs of Energy were below 2%, and essentially stable; today, ECoEs are above 11%, and are rising relentlessly." Published author (*Life After Growth*). |
| Reader Appeal | 3 | Dense but rewarding for interested readers. Unique "two economies" framework (material vs. financial) genuinely differs from mainstream economic discourse. |
| Brand Safety | 5 | No inflammatory language. Heterodox perspective but focused on energy data and economic modeling, not partisan politics. |
| Fediverse Fit | 3 | Single author, personal voice (sometimes sardonic). More essayist than conversational, but a real person with a distinctive analytical framework. |
| Visual Polish | 3 | Standard WordPress.com theme. Functional. |
| **Total** | **18** | |

Stats: 29,192 30-day views, 1,091 subscribers, 5 posts in 2026, last post Apr 2.

## 5. ~~Lars P. Syll~~ (larspsyll.wordpress.com) -- 11/25 REPLACED

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 2 | Very thin. Economics posts are mostly block quotes from others with brief commentary. 118 posts in 2026 sounds prolific, but most are extremely short. Many posts are personal/music content in Swedish. |
| Reader Appeal | 2 | High frequency, low substance. Swedish-language posts are inaccessible to most readers. Narrow focus (anti-mainstream methodology critique on repeat). |
| Brand Safety | 1 | **Red flag.** "Trump's little toyboy" uses crude, demeaning language. Political posts consistently use derogatory language without substance. Not thoughtful academic discourse. |
| Fediverse Fit | 3 | Has personal voice and frequent posting, but veers between cranky academic, political provocateur, and Swedish music enthusiast. Inconsistent identity. |
| Visual Polish | 3 | Standard WordPress.com theme. Functional. |

**Verdict: Replaced.** Brand safety issues are disqualifying. Content quality is poor. Lowest subscriber count in the pack (597) confirms low resonance.

### Replacement: searching

The economics/ideas blog landscape on WordPress is thin. No strong individual blogger replacement found yet. Options: (a) keep the pack at 4 blogs with 3 strong performers + Surplus Energy Economics, or (b) broaden the "Ideas" scope.
