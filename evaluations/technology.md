# Technology & Science Starter Pack Evaluation

Evaluated: April 13, 2026

## Scoring Rubric

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 20-25 | Strong pick, prioritize for outreach |
| 15-19 | Acceptable, keep unless a better option exists |
| 10-14 | Weak, look for replacement |
| Below 10 | Replace immediately |

## 1. Out of Office Hours (oofhours.com) -- 23/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 5 | Deeply original. The sysprep post traces decades of history with firsthand experience. Genuine practitioner expertise on Windows deployment, MDT, Intune. Michael Niehaus writes from real-world experience. |
| Reader Appeal | 4 | Essential reading for Windows IT/enterprise deployment. Niche but very valuable to its audience. Engaging, opinionated writing. |
| Brand Safety | 5 | Clean. Professional IT content. |
| Fediverse Fit | 5 | Strong personal voice ("technology ramblings" tagline). Conversational, rhetorical questions, wry humor. Model fediverse blog. |
| Visual Polish | 4 | Clean WordPress theme, good typography (Newsreader font), responsive. Professional without being flashy. |

**Verdict: Keep. Best blog in the pack.**

## 2. j2sw Blog / Packets Down Range (blog.j2sw.com) -- 21/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 4 | Deep network engineering content (BGP, TCP-AO, data center design, ISP operations). Mix of technical deep-dives, product notes, and professional philosophy pieces ("When Competence Is Unnoticed"). |
| Reader Appeal | 4 | For network engineers and ISP operators, this is a goldmine. 41 posts in 2026 means frequent, fresh content. |
| Brand Safety | 5 | Clean. Professional networking content. |
| Fediverse Fit | 5 | Strong personal voice. Justin Wilson is clearly a real practitioner sharing from experience. Personal touches (49" monitor review, 3D printing) mixed with technical content. "Packets Down Range" branding is memorable. |
| Visual Polish | 3 | Functional WordPress theme. Nothing special visually. Some pages may have rendering issues with heavy JavaScript. |

**Verdict: Keep.** Hidden gem despite only 72 subscribers. The 9,453 views and 41 posts in 2026 show real engagement.

## 3. HACKMAGEDDON (hackmageddon.com) -- 18/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 4 | Original compilation and statistical analysis of cyber attacks. Paolo Passeri's biweekly timelines are genuine curation work. The AI malware piece aggregates 23 incidents with sources. |
| Reader Appeal | 3 | Useful for security professionals tracking threats. But the repetitive format (timeline, statistics, timeline) can feel monotonous. Occasional thematic posts add variety. |
| Brand Safety | 5 | Clean. Factual cybersecurity reporting. |
| Fediverse Fit | 2 | Fundamentally a reference resource, not a personal blog. Institutional, data-driven voice. Posts read like database entries rather than conversations. |
| Visual Polish | 4 | Clean OceanWP theme, dark header, grid layout with featured images. Professional. |

**Verdict: Keep (watch).** Solid cybersecurity resource but weak fediverse fit. Functions better as a bookmark than a feed subscription.

## 4. Promega Connections (promegaconnections.com) -- 17/25

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Content Quality | 4 | Surprisingly good science writing. Cancer/aging piece is genuinely informative. Rabbit DNA metabarcoding piece shows range. Product mentions are minimal (~15%) and naturally integrated. |
| Reader Appeal | 3 | Interesting for science readers. Mix of cancer research, wildlife ecology, science history. But some posts are more promotional (Maxwell RSC, BRETSA Target Engagement). |
| Brand Safety | 4 | It IS a corporate blog. Automattic showcasing another company's marketing blog could look odd. Content is educational rather than salesy. |
| Fediverse Fit | 2 | Corporate blog with rotating authors. No consistent personal voice. Not community-oriented. Good content marketing, but poor fit for fediverse ethos of individual voices. |
| Visual Polish | 4 | Professional design with navy/orange color scheme. Clean typography. Well-organized navigation. |

**Verdict: Keep (watch).** Good science writing undermined by being a corporate marketing blog. If an independent science blogger with a personal WordPress blog surfaces, consider replacing.
